## Solving Technique

These are the methods used to solve a Sudoku. There are many different solving techniques discovered in the last few years, mainly because there is a large community of puzzlers who share their knowledge on the various [Sudoku Forums](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Sudoku_Forums).

Because there are so many, a rough subclassification has been made:

## Single Digit Patterns

[Skyscraper](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Skyscraper)Two parallel strong links, weakly connected at the base.[2-String Kite](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/2-String_Kite)Two crossing strong links, weakly connected in a box.[Empty Rectangle](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Empty_Rectangle)A single-digit technique that makes use of a [box](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Box) whose [candidates](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Candidate) for that [digit](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Digit) are contained within the union of a [boxrow](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Boxrow) and a [boxcol](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Boxcol).

## Uniqueness

[Uniqueness Test](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Uniqueness_Test)A set of techniques that avoids the [Unique Rectangle](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Unique_Rectangle)[deadly pattern](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Deadly_pattern).[Avoidable Rectangle](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Avoidable_Rectangle)A set of techniques that avoids the [Unique Rectangle](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Unique_Rectangle)[deadly pattern](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Deadly_pattern). Perhaps unique in solving techniques in that it employs solved [cells](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Cell) as well as those unsolved.[Bivalue Universal Grave](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Bivalue_Universal_Grave)A set of techniques that avoids the [Bivalue Universal Grave](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Bivalue_Universal_Grave)[deadly pattern](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Deadly_pattern).[BUG Lite](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/BUG_Lite)A set of techniques that avoids the [BUG Lite](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/BUG_Lite)[deadly pattern](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Deadly_pattern).[Uniqueness Controversy](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Uniqueness_Controversy)These solving techniques assume that the [Sudoku](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Sudoku) puzzle has a unique solution. It is not universally accepted that this assumption is a valid one.
## [Chains](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Chain) and [Loops](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Loop)

[Forcing Chain](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Forcing_Chain)Generic term for any type of chain.[X-Chain](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/X-Chain)Single digit chain of cells.[XY-Chain](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/XY-Chain)Chain of [bivalue](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Bivalue) cells.[Remote Pairs](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Remote_Pairs)Simplified form of XY-Chain, involving only two digits.[Fishy Cycle](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Fishy_Cycle) | X-Cycle Single digit [continuous](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Continuous) loop.[Broken Wing](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Broken_Wing)Eliminations caused by loops of odd length.[Nice Loops](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Nice_Loop)Several types of loops formed by cells following strict rules and a notation system.[Double Implication Chain](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Double_Implication_Chain) | DIC There are 2 interpretations circulating. The first originates from a reliable source.
*   A Forcing Chain that has implications in both directions.
*   2 Forcing Chains starting from a bivalue cell or a [bilocal](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Bilocal) unit showing a [verity](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Verity).

[Alternating Inference Chain](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Alternating_Inference_Chain) | AIC A chain where each node is a candidate, with alternating strong and weak inference.[Oriented Chains](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Oriented_Chains)Oriented chains are generalisations of the basic xy-chains. The central idea is that the information collected from previous candidates in a chain (and/or from the target) can be used to select the next candidates. Oriented chains are either 2D or 3D:- oriented 2D chains: xyt-chains, xyz-chains and xyzt-chains, together with their "hidden" counterparts: hxyt-chains, hxyz-chains and hxyzt-chains;- [oriented 3D chains](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Oriented_3D_chains): nrct-chains, nrcz-chains, nrczt-chains.(The original reference to all these chains is the book "The Hidden Logic of Sudoku". Further web references forthcoming)
## Wings

[XY-Wing](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/XY-Wing)Three [cells](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Cell) with [pivot](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Pivot) cell **XY** and two [pincer](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Pincer) cells **XZ** and **YZ**.[XYZ-Wing](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/XYZ-Wing)Three [cells](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Cell) with [pivot](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Pivot) cell **XYZ** and two [pincer](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Pincer) cells **XZ** and **YZ**.[WXYZ-Wing](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/WXYZ-Wing)Four [cells](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Cell) with [pivot](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Pivot) cell **WXYZ** and three [pincer](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Pincer) cells **WZ**, **XZ** and **YZ**.[W-Wing](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/W-Wing)Four [cells](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Cell) in a [chain](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Chain): a cell **WX**, a cell with **X** as a [candidate](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Candidate), another cell with **X** as a [candidate](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Candidate), another cell **WX**, such that the two cells containing **X** as a [candidate](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Candidate) have a [strong link](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Strong_link).
## [Almost Locked Sets](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Almost_Locked_Set)

[ALS-XZ](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/ALS-XZ) rule 2 Almost Locked Sets with [restricted common](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Restricted_common) digit **X** perform eliminations for common digit **Z**.[ALS-XY-Wing](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/ALS-XY-Wing) rule 3 Almost Locked Sets with 2 restricted common digits **Y** and **Z** perform eliminations for common digit **X**.[ALS-XY-Chain](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/ALS-XY-Chain)Very similar to [XY-Chain](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/XY-Chain), except that the [nodes](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Node) in the [chain](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Chain) are Almost Locked Sets.[Death Blossom](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Death_Blossom)A stem cell of N candidates pointing to N petals, each an Almost Locked Set.

## Miscellaneous

[Sue de Coq](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Sue_de_Coq)Uses two intersecting sets **A** and **B**, where **A** is a set of N cells with N candidates in a line, **B** is a set of N cells with N candidates in a box, and the sets **A - B** and **B - A** have no common candidates.[Subset Counting](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Subset_Counting) | [Extended Subset Principle](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Extended_Subset_Principle)Considers the number of times a digit can be placed in a selected subset of cells.[Braid Analysis](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Braid_Analysis) | [Braiding](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Braiding) | [Traveling Pairs](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Traveling_Pairs)Analyzes the distribution of digits in a [chute](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Chute), especially how pairs repeat in a chute.[Constraint Subsets](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Constraint_Subsets)Generalized view of [subset](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Subset) and [fish](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Fish) techniques.[Equivalence Marks](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Equivalence_Marks)Similar to [coloring](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Coloring), but uses marks instead of colors to represent parity.[Gurth's Symmetrical Placement](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Gurth%27s_Symmetrical_Placement)Technique for puzzles with rotational symmetry.
## Techniques of Last Resort

Most of these techniques are controversial. They are either too complex to be used by human solvers, or they require a lot of extra work, or they are not based on logic.

[Forcing Net](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Forcing_Net)A general term for techniques that investigate branching chains.[Tabling](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Tabling) | Trebor's Tables All implications for each move are collected in tables.[Graded Equivalence Marks](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Graded_Equivalence_Marks) | GEM A system of marking candidates starting from 2 complementary starting positions.[Bowman Bingo](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Bowman_Bingo)A systematic approach to investigate implications.[Trial & Error](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Trial_%26_Error) | [Ariadne's Thread](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Ariadne%27s_Thread) | [Bifurcation](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Bifurcation)The implications for a single move are investigated.[Nishio](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Nishio)Trial & Error limited to single digit.[Templating](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Templating) | [Pattern Overlay Method](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Pattern_Overlay_Method) | POM All possible ways to place a digit in the remaining candidate space are investigated.[Guessing](https://www.sudopedia.org/wiki/Guess)Random moves are made, without any logic behind them.
