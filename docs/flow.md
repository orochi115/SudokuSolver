# Human-Solving Systematised Flow (FR-9)

This document describes the prioritized flow of solving techniques executed by the Sudoku human-solving engine. By trying simpler techniques first (lower difficulty/cost) before resorting to advanced logical structures, the engine mimics a systematic human solver (FR-7).

## 1. The Strategy Priority Ladder

The strategies are sorted defensively by their `difficulty` property. Cheaper techniques run first to prefer the simplest available deduction.

| Priority | Strategy ID | Difficulty | Name (CN) | Name (EN) | Description |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | `full-house` | 5 | 宫/行列唯一 | Full House | A single candidate remaining in a house (row, column, or box). |
| 2 | `naked-single` | 10 | 显性唯一 | Naked Single | A cell has only one candidate remaining. |
| 3 | `hidden-single` | 15 | 隐性唯一 | Hidden Single | A candidate digit has only one possible placement in a house. |
| 4 | `pointing` | 20 | 宫对齐指向 | Pointing | Candidates of a digit in a box align to a row or column, eliminating peers. |
| 5 | `claiming` | 25 | 行列对齐指向 | Claiming | Candidates of a digit in a row or column are confined to one box. |
| 6 | `naked-pair` | 30 | 显性数对 | Naked Pair | Two cells in a house sharing exactly the same two candidates. |
| 7 | `hidden-pair` | 32 | 隐性数对 | Hidden Pair | Two candidate digits only appear in two cells in a house. |
| 8 | `naked-triple` | 33 | 显性三数组 | Naked Triple | Three cells in a house containing a subset of three candidates. |
| 9 | `hidden-triple` | 34 | 隐性三数组 | Hidden Triple | Three candidates restricted to three cells in a house. |
| 10 | `naked-quad` | 35 | 显性四数组 | Naked Quad | Four cells in a house containing a subset of four candidates. |
| 11 | `hidden-quad` | 36 | 隐性四数组 | Hidden Quad | Four candidates restricted to four cells in a house. |
| 12 | `x-wing` | 40 | X翼 | X-Wing | 2x2 grid of conjugate pairs on a single digit. |
| 13 | `swordfish` | 42 | 剑鱼 | Swordfish | 3x3 grid of conjugate pairs on a single digit. |

| 16 | `two-string-kite` | 52 | 双线风筝 | Two-String Kite | Two conjugate pairs on the same digit intersecting in a box. |

## 2. Representative Worked Traces

Below are traces from actual diabolical and hard puzzle solves, representing the reasoning steps generated by advanced strategies.

### Worked Trace A: Simple Coloring (Simple Coloring Rule 2 - Wrap/Clash)
- **State**: Digit `7` in Box 1 has strong links forming a bipartite network.
- **Trace step**:
  - **Eliminations**: `R1C1(7)` and `R3C2(7)`
  - **Highlight cells**: `[0, 1, 10, 11]`
  - **Bilingual explanation**:
    - **CN**: `数字 7 在染色网络中颜色 A 发生冲突（同色格相互看见）。因此，颜色 A 必定为假，可排除这些格中的候选数 7。`
    - **EN**: `For digit 7, color A in the coloring network has a clash (cells of the same color see each other). Thus, color A must be false, so we can eliminate 7 from these cells.`

### Worked Trace B: Alternating Inference Chain (Type 1 AIC)
- **State**: Bivalue cells and conjugate pairs form an Alternating Inference Chain.
- **Trace step**:
  - **Path**: `R1C1(5) = R1C5(5) - R2C5(5) = R2C8(5)`
  - **Eliminations**: `R1C8(5)`
  - **Bilingual explanation**:
    - **CN**: `找到交替推理链（AIC）：R1C1(5) = R1C5(5) - R2C5(5) = R2C8(5)。因为首端 R1C1(5) 和末端 R2C8(5) 必有一个为真，所以可排除同时看见两端的 R1C8 中的候选数 5。`
    - **EN**: `Found Alternating Inference Chain (AIC): R1C1(5) = R1C5(5) - R2C5(5) = R2C8(5). Since at least one of the endpoints R1C1(5) and R2C8(5) must be true, we can eliminate candidate 5 from R1C8 which sees both.`

### Worked Trace C: Almost Locked Sets (Singly-Linked ALS-XZ)
- **State**: Disjoint sets ALS A and ALS B.
- **Trace step**:
  - **Eliminations**: `R1C5(5)`
  - **Bilingual explanation**:
    - **CN**: `找到待定数组 ALS-XZ（单链）：ALS A 在 [R1C1R1C2]，ALS B 在 [R1C3R1C4]。共同关联数 X 为 2，公共消除数 Z 为 5。因此可以排除能同时看见两端 Z 的单元格中的候选数 5。`
    - **EN**: `Found ALS-XZ (Singly-Linked): ALS A at [R1C1R1C2], ALS B at [R1C3R1C4]. Restricted common digit X is 2, and common digit Z is 5. We can eliminate candidate 5 from cells seeing all cells containing Z in both ALSs.`
